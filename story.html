A Bird and A Star

English· November 2023· Space Club Pick Award — Temple University, Japan Campus
"When the stars set themselves in a pattern, people desperately try to find a sign. But in the process, something gets irrevocably lost."

Many things happen at college libraries. It's a chaotic hive with constant movements. Life manifests itself through every corner, even if you would not think about it at first glance. Someone sleeps on their backpack. In another corner, a head is laying at the table, constricting the blood flow in the neck. They'll have a headache when they wake up. Someone over there studies. Someone applies for a college transfer after a disgraceful grade on a midterm.

And someone goes to the library at the same exact time every day to sit at the same exact seats. Once seated, they don't leave the place until they have to return home. There are no signs of eating or drinking. No sleeping either. What does this guy even live off? Photosynthesis??!!

Another student walks in. She carries a purse and a backpack. She takes a lap around the library. The library at Temple University Japan is small, only one floor, and can be walked around in a matter of a minute. The student is looking for something. An empty seat? But she already passed a number of empty ones. Maybe she's looking for someone? Eventually, she settles across the window. Today's weather is pleasant, and the golden sun rays peak through the blinds. They fall on the leather notebook with the name Evangelina written on it in precise elegant handwriting. It was as flawless as a computer font.

Everything about Evangelina screamed 'perfection'. She held her head up, but not in a pretentious manner. Her posture was elegant and looked effortless, as if she was made like that, carved from a steel material that never made her back curve like a chess piece knight while seated behind the desk. She was always very precise in the path she was walking, always avoiding people, and therefore never bumping into one. Fast-reactive reflexes and amazing body coordination.

Today, while doing her routine walk, for the first time she bumped into someone. Evangelina has been doing her circuits around the library in the particular patterns of time intervals. Never has that been an issue before. This girl should not have been in her way. But she was, standing like a ghost, like a possessed creature, staring with her deep eyes at the open pages of Evangelina's notebook. She dropped it in the process, and the pages treacherously turned themselves on peculiar drawings. As if struck by lightning, the interloper bent over in an attempt to help Evangelina to pick up her stuff, not taking her eyes off the notebook.

"So you can see them too?" whispered the girl under her breath, looking up.

"I do not know what you're talking about," Evangelina hurried to close the notebook attempting to draw the attention away from the subject.

However, all this facade turns into dust when the girl, with a sigh of relief, continued,

"The energy points. You know about them too. I thought I was crazy."

Well, she might be. What she refers to are called convergence points—the points where the strings that make up the Universe according to the String Theory converge and cause resonance. It is a leak of energy from the otherwise perfect system. It explains the chaos that fills our existence. These points are a very potent source of energy. Although it is exclusive to a living matter to interact with these points, people usually can't see them. These clusters of energy even Evangelina, Model V-640-210, could only see as faint traces captured by her intricately designed sensors.

The interloper girl's name was Catherine. She had black colorless hair. Colorless because it did not retain any saturation, as if all life faded away from it. The absence of all the colors. A dark void, the same that filled her eyes. Her striking eyeliner was also just as black. Her black turtleneck looked too tight, almost as if suffocating her. A raven embroidered with silver string rested on her long chiffon skirt. Catherine looked strange, but she looked human. Her movements had that just 'slightly off' flair to them that Evangelina always lacked. It's ironic how a perfect device lacks in the only area—having a flaw.

"Tell me about what you figured out so far." Evangelina requested once they both went to a less quiet area, but still quiet enough so you don't have to scream on top of your lungs to make sense of what the person sitting across from you is saying. They needed a place where the people around would not pay attention to their talking—the cafeteria of the neighboring Japanese Showa Women's University. Besides, it was Catherine's initiative, so who was Evangelina to say 'no'.

"Well, you noticed that guy, right? The one who always sits either in the right-most corner, in the one near the wall divider across the window, or the seat in the very front. Have you noticed anything particular about his relocations?"

"I have a hunch, yes."

"It's all about the convergence points!" Catherine said this with a smile on her face while clapping once. She was very proud of herself for knowing it. Her eyes even showed a subtle sign of a spark. It's as if her entire purpose in life was concentrated in these convergence points. She had this psychic-like fascination about them. But all of this quickly vanished and she returned to her emo-self, as she suddenly changed the subject and asked,

"Are you gonna eat? There is something about the miso ramen at this place that keeps me eating it day after day. The same thing. Maybe I am being pre-programmed for that?" This was said as a sarcastic joke with a straight face. Catherine might have looked like she's always detached from the world with her gothic self-expression, but she was actually a warm person on the inside. Indeed, she was even kind of hurt when her outfit and makeup were confused for a Halloween costume. That was just how she dressed, experimenting with her looks, but always sticking to the same color palette.

Evangelina, however, always followed the same routine in everything she was doing, so a lighthearted comment about a human-being following a known algorithmic routine only made her feel called out.

That's right, she's a robot. She can't eat. And that's a bit of a problem, since Catherine sits here, right across from her, looking with her empty void eyes. They have just met, so she has to show her best impression of a human. Not like she didn't have years of experience in testing. And then about a year ago she was dropped off at Temple University Japan, where the faint traces of convergence points were noticed. She had a trial period of getting accustomed to humans in an everyday setting. Now her training was supposed to come to an end, but she still hasn't heard anything from the higher ups. She can't collect the energy from the convergence points, but she tried to map them out, preparing for the day they will send her the test tube with synthesized organic matter for the energy capture.

"No, I think I'm good."

✧ ✦ ✧

Today something very ordinary happened, something inherent to any college library that has careless students in it. That means, just any student. Catherine was sitting alone, mapping out the patterns of the faint light traces of the convergence points and matching them with the star locations, when someone forgot to plug in their headphones. The quietness of the library was struck with a loud male voice, reciting an audiobook, "Dazai Osamu. No Longer Human. Ningen Shikkaku. Notebook 1," before a guy in the right-most corner feverishly plugged his headphones in.

'No Longer Human.'

'Ningen Shikkaku.'

These words echoed in Catherine's head, until she suddenly felt overwhelmed and now joined the company of the knocked-out students resting their heads at the library desks. It felt as if these words reminded her of something, archived deep down in her memory. But now, all the blurry memories rushed into her head, flooding and drowning her consciousness. 'No Longer Human. Ningen Shikkaku', became the silent screams of her cognition.

She suddenly woke up with a feeling of desperation and a striking realization. Breathing heavily, she rushed into a bathroom to wash her face with cold water. She could not believe what she remembered. But now, everything made sense: the occasional twitching of her eye, lost sensation on the outer part of her right pinky finger, the absence of undertones in her hair, and, most importantly, the visions of tiny rays of overpowering light that emerge through the earth—the convergence points finally made sense.

She is human. Well, she started off as one. Now, she isn't, not completely. She was created as a part of a new energy research program to detect the convergence points that supposedly could become an alternative source of energy. As was discovered, only a living matter can properly harvest it, so to oppose an Astraellar Co., a rival company decided to create cyborgs. To fuse a living human with a soulless machine. They named their creation after Japanese yōkai: kitsune, tanuki, tengu… Tengu. A bird-like spirit, sometimes illustrated to have a body of a crow.

Tengu. If you try to type it on a traditional Japanese phone keyboard, you will have to push number 4 four times to get te. Then you press 0 three times to get n. And finally, you press 2 three times and a star for a dakuten to get gu. If you substitute a star with 0 for a numerical value, you'll get 44-03-230. Catherine viciously searched for a small mirror and checked her neck.

Model T-44-03-230.

The reason she did not remember she was not fully human is because the program was discontinued. Both rival companies failed, since it proved to be impractical to utilize the convergence points, just as we can't use fusion nuclear energy on a reasonable scale. Without any software updates, the program initiative went into hibernation within the subconscious. The words 'Ningen Shikkaku' served as the code to activate it again. Makes it question if it indeed was a harmless accident on the library guy's end, or a meticulously planned scheme.

Catherine now realized something that she hadn't noticed before. Evangelina wasn't a human either. She was a robot device created by Astraellar Co., and since it was shut down, she'll never receive the command to proceed. No samples will be sent anywhere. Her existence is practically useless.

Engulfed with panic, Catherine rushed through the hallway in search of Evangelina, struggling to catch her breath. She opened the door of an empty classroom and saw the humanoid friend. Evangelina reacted calmly to her hysterical friend. But then, Catherine charged at her and with a nearly incomprehensible whisper said, "We are both neglected pieces of junk, and they are coming for us."

✧ ✦ ✧

Sometimes it all comes to this. You believe in one thing your entire life, and in an instant it disappears. Your sole reason for life is being washed away like a drawing on sand. The water covers it, destroys, and never brings back. The same can happen with an intricate device. One day, a metal part washes off the shore. Through the deterioration you can hardly read the text.

Model V-640-210.

The star Vega shone as bright as ever the night the immortal body strikingly fell into the nearest canal.

A note on the title. A tengu is a bird-like yōkai often drawn with the body of a crow; on an old Japanese phone keypad its kana spell out the model number stamped on a cyborg's neck — and the star, struck for a dakuten, is what gives the spirit its name. A bird, and a star.
