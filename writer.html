Weaver's Demise

Shards of words create a kaleidoscope of stories.

Writing as А. Velvet Russian & English · studying Japanese
weaversdemise.wordpress.com ↗ Instagram weavers.demise@gmail.com
Silkworms weave their cocoons,
then are killed inside them
to make the silk.
Their cradle becomes their coffin.

The name Weaver's Demise holds that image — a making that costs the maker. It's a powerful metaphor that goes well beyond the death of a silkworm.

I'm a Russian author who moved to the U.S. in high school. My work ranges from poetry and music to short stories and novels — in English and Russian, with Japanese on the way. This is where the science sets down its instruments and the writing picks up.
