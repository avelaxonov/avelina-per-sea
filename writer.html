Weaver's Cradle

Shards of words create a kaleidoscope of stories.

Writes in Russian & English · studying Japanese & Arabic · Ukrainian
«The words of the poet have gentle embrace.»— from Weaver’s Demise
The silkworm weaves its cocoon—a cradle—not knowing if it’ll turn into a coffin.

The name Weaver's Cradle reads first as a beginning — the cradle where ideas, stories, and creativity are born. Yet the cocoon a silkworm spins is its cradle and, in sericulture, the place it never leaves. The same object holds opposite roles; the tragedy stays folded inside the image, for the reader to discover. Creation that already contains its ending — beauty made through sacrifice.

I'm a bilingual writer — Russian and English — who moved to the U.S. at fifteen. My work ranges from poetry and music to short stories and novels, in both languages, with Japanese and Arabic on the way. This is where the science sets down its instruments and the writing picks up.
